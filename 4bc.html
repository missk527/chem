Chemical Equations: Word & Symbol Forms + Balancing

Key Notes (rewritten from the speech)

1) Why chemical equations?
  • They show what happens in a chemical reaction.
  • Two common forms: word equations and symbol (formula) equations.
2) Word vs. Symbol equations
  • Word equation example: “Methane + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water”.
  • Symbol equation uses chemical formulae: CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
  • Left side: reactants. Right side: products. The arrow shows the reaction proceeds to products.
3) Diatomic elements (use the correct formulas)
  • Some elements exist naturally as diatomic molecules: O₂ (oxygen), Cl₂ (chlorine), N₂ (nitrogen).
  • Therefore, write O₂ (not O), Cl₂ (not Cl), N₂ (not N) in equations.
4) Balancing rule: conservation of atoms
  • A balanced equation has the same number of each type of atom on both sides of the arrow.
  • Balancing is often trial and error—adjust and recheck until counts match.
5) What you can and can’t change
  • Never change the small numbers in formulas (subscripts)—that would make a different substance (e.g., O₂ ≠ O₃).
  • You may change only the big numbers out front (coefficients): they tell how many units of a substance are involved.
  • Keep coefficients as whole numbers (no fractions like 1½ O₂).
6) Example 1: Methane combustion
Unbalanced: CH4 + O2 → CO2 + H2O Counts LHS: C=1, H=4, O=2 Counts RHS: C=1, H=2, O=3 ← not balanced Step 1: Make O even on LHS → 2 O2 (gives 4 O atoms). Step 2: Fix H and O on RHS → 2 H2O. Balanced: CH4 + 2 O2 → CO2 + 2 H2O

Now each side has C=1, H=4, O=4 — perfectly balanced.

7) Example 2: Acid–base (H₂SO₄ + NaOH)
Word: Sulfuric acid + sodium hydroxide → sodium sulfate + water Start: H2SO4 + NaOH → Na2SO4 + H2O Check: H=3, S=1, O=5, Na=1 (LHS) vs H=2, S=1, O=5, Na=2 (RHS) Tip: Balance the least common elements first (e.g., Na, S). Step 1: Put 2 in front of NaOH → H2SO4 + 2 NaOH → Na2SO4 + H2O Now: LHS H=4, S=1, O=6, Na=2; RHS H=2, S=1, O=5, Na=2 Step 2: Add one more water on RHS → 2 H2O Balanced: H2SO4 + 2 NaOH → Na2SO4 + 2 H2O Check: H=4, S=1, O=6, Na=2 on both sides ✓
8) Key terms

Reactants — starting substances. Products — substances formed. Coefficient — big number in front. Subscript — small number inside a formula.
