Separating Solids from Liquids Filtration · Evaporation · Crystallization

Notes (Exam-ready key points)

Key terminology
  • Insoluble solid + liquid (e.g., sand in water) → a mixture of a liquid and an insoluble solid.
  • Soluble solid + liquid (e.g., sodium chloride in water) → a solution.
  • In a solution: the solute is the substance that dissolves; the solvent is the liquid that does the dissolving.
Filtration (insoluble solid from liquid)
  • Like using a kitchen sieve; in labs we use filter paper (tiny pores) often in a filter funnel.
  • Liquid passes through the paper; the insoluble solid is left behind on the paper.
  • Filtration does not separate a soluble solid from a liquid (solutions pass through).
Evaporation (soluble solid from solution)
  • Place the solution in an evaporating dish (or crucible) on a tripod and heat gently with a Bunsen burner.
  • The solvent evaporates → solution becomes more concentrated → crystals form → eventually dry crystals remain.
  • Pros: relatively quick and easy.
  • Con: some solids can decompose on heating (thermal decomposition).
Crystallization (for heat‑sensitive solids)
  • Use when the solute might thermally decompose if strongly heated.
  • Steps: heat the solution gently (often in a water bath) until crystals just start to form → stop heating and allow to cool.
  • As it cools, solubility decreases → more crystals form.
  • Filter the crystals from the remaining solution, then dry them (warm place or oven).
