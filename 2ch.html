Atoms, Isotopes & Relative Atomic Mass

Key Notes

1) Structure of the Atom

An atom has a nucleus with protons and neutrons, and electrons orbiting it. The proton number (atomic number, Z) determines the element.

  • Hydrogen: 1 proton, 1 electron.
  • Helium: 2 protons, 2 neutrons, 2 electrons.
2) Element Symbols

Symbols use 1–2 letters. Examples: C=Carbon, Li=Lithium, Na=Sodium, Fe=Iron.

3) Isotopes

Isotopes = same protons, different neutrons. Chemical properties same, mass differs.

  • Carbon‑12: 6p, 6n, 6e.
  • Carbon‑13: 6p, 7n, 6e.
4) Relative Atomic Mass (Ar)

Weighted average of isotope mass numbers and abundances.

Ar = Σ(isotope abundance × isotope mass) / Σ(abundances)

Example (copper): (69.2×63 + 30.8×65) / (69.2 + 30.8) = 63.6 (1 d.p.)

5) Exam Tips
  • Element identity depends only on protons.
  • Mass number (≈ p + n) distinguishes isotopes.
  • When using %, denominator is 100; otherwise, sum of fractional abundances is 1.
