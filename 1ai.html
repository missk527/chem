Chemistry

Key Notes

Atoms: the tiny particles of matter
  • Everything (living and non‑living) is made of atoms; a single cell contains an enormous number (well over 1014).
  • An atom has a central nucleus with protons and neutrons, and electrons that move around it in shells (energy levels).
Relative mass & charge of subatomic particles
  • Proton: charge = +1; relative mass = 1.
  • Neutron: charge = 0 (neutral); relative mass = 1.
  • Electron: charge = −1; relative mass ≈ 1/2000 (often taken as ~0).
Size & scale
  • Typical atomic radius ≈ 0.1 nm (1×10−10 m). Most of an atom is empty space.
  • The nucleus is about 10,000× smaller than the atom; electrons are even smaller (too small to draw to scale).
Neutral atoms & ions
  • If protons = electrons → overall charge 0 (neutral atom).
  • Gain electrons → more negatives → a negative ion (e.g., 1−, 2−).
  • Lose electrons → more positives → a positive ion (e.g., 1+, 2+).
Periodic table & nuclear symbols
  • Each box is an element (a type of atom) with its element symbol (e.g., O for oxygen, Li for lithium).
  • Atomic number (Z) = number of protons (also electrons in a neutral atom).
  • Mass number (A) = total protons + neutrons.

Examples: Oxygen has Z = 8 and A = 16 → neutrons = 16 − 8 = 8. Lithium has Z = 3 and A = 7 → neutrons = 4.
